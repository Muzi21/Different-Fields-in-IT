What is Cyber Security?

Cyber Security refers to the practice of protecting computer systems, networks, and digital data from unauthorized access, cyberattacks, and other forms of damage or exploitation. It encompasses various technologies, processes, and practices designed to safeguard computers, servers, mobile devices, electronic systems, networks, and data from malicious activities.

Key components of Cyber Security:

  • Network Security: Protecting computer networks from unauthorized access, intrusion, and malicious activities through technologies such as firewalls, intrusion detection systems (IDS), and virtual private networks (VPNs).
  • Endpoint Security: Securing individual devices (such as computers, smartphones, and tablets) from cyber threats with measures like antivirus software, encryption, and device management tools.
  • Data Security: Safeguarding sensitive data (such as personal information, financial records, and intellectual property) through encryption, access controls, data loss prevention (DLP) solutions, and secure storage practices.
  • Application Security: Ensuring the security of software applications and web applications by identifying and addressing vulnerabilities, implementing secure coding practices, and using tools like application firewalls and penetration testing.

Common Cyber Threats:

  • Malware: Discover what malware is and how it can infect your devices, steal data, and disrupt operations
  • Phishing: Recognize phishing attempts and learn how to avoid falling victim to fraudulent emails, texts, and calls.
  • Ransomware: Understand the devastating impact of ransomware attacks and how they hold data hostage for extortion

Cyber Security Best Practices:

  • Password Security: Master the art of creating strong, unique passwords and managing them securely.
  • Software Updates: Stay protected by regularly updating your operating systems, applications, and antivirus software.
  • Data Encryption: Learn about encryption and how it can protect your sensitive information from unauthorized access.
